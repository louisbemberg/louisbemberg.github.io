About me

I was born in Lausanne, Switzerland. I grew up playing competitive golf while studying mathematics and physics. After representing the Swiss Golf National team in international tournaments, I was offered a scholarship to go play college golf in Texas. I graduated as the valedictorian of my class with a Bachelor of Science in Industrial Engineering, and had the honor to receive the award of the best student of my class. After that, I decided to move to Madrid to learn Spanish, a great goal of mine since I could not communicate with the Venezuelan portion of my family. I got hired as an unpaid intern in one of the biggest innovation & entrepreneurship conferences in Europe, and eventually became the Head of Program there. After 2 years, I decided to leave my job to pursue a coding bootcamp, Le Wagon, to learn how to code and to be able to build my own projects. My current goal is to find a job in the tech industry to keep building products and solving problems while continuing my journey of learning how to code.
